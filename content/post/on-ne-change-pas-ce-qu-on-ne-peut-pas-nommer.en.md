"Why does nothing change, when everyone wants change?"

Budgets, tools, training programs. And still, it stalls.

The usual explanation, "we're pulling the wrong levers", is still too optimistic: it assumes there is a right lever, somewhere, that we simply haven't found.

There is something deeper than levers: **the mental toolkit**, the stock of words, categories and reference points available in an organization, which determines not what we think, but what is thinkable.

You can't see it from the inside, just as you can't hear your own mother tongue.

"We've always done it this way" is therefore not resistance -> it's the sign that no other option has a name.

AI makes the whole thing even more burning, but not in the way people say.
It doesn't fail the transformation: it reshapes the toolkit. It learns to convert any situation into a well-formulated problem.

What gets trained is framing.
What atrophies is judgement about what has no formulation yet, and a machine that can only process the already-formulated will never name anything on our behalf.

You can't change what you can't name. That's where the work begins - not in the tools.
